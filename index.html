Algorithms

What is an algorithm?

Algorithms. We hear about them all the time. Maybe your friend tweeted "I hate insta's new algorithm!!!" or maybe you blamed not favoriting your mother's facebook posts because "the damn algorithm!", but do you really know and understand what an algorithm is? Most of us don't. Let's start at the beginningâ€” Miriam Webster defines an Algorithm as "a step by step procedure for solving a problem or accomplishing some end on a computer." The kind of algorithm we're talking about and oftentimes the one of popular discussion is that of post order on social media. The creators of social media write code for the order of posts on your timeline, newsfeed, dashboard, etc. to best suit what they've perceived as your interests. As you might guess, this can get dangerous.

Why would developers want to use them?

App developers have their own reasons for utilizing such algorithms based on what they believe you'll like to see. For starters, the more you like the posts you're seeing, the more likely they think you will be to return to the app. In a developer's eyes, if you don'y like the content, then you don't like the app. Sometimes this is true, but sometimes it leads to you only seeing one type of post and not getting to experience it all.
